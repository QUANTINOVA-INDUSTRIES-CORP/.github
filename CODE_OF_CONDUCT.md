# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

QUANTINOVA INDUSTRIES CORP is committed to providing a professional, respectful, harassment-free environment for everyone participating in our projects and technical communities, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We commit to acting and interacting in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Expected behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- demonstrating empathy, patience, and respect;
- communicating clearly and professionally;
- giving and accepting constructive, evidence-based feedback;
- acknowledging mistakes and taking corrective action;
- respecting different viewpoints, experiences, roles, and responsibilities;
- focusing discussion on the work, requirements, risks, and outcomes;
- protecting confidential, personal, security-sensitive, and proprietary information;
- complying with applicable law, corporate policy, repository rules, and professional standards.

## Unacceptable behavior

Unacceptable behavior includes:

- sexualized language or imagery, unwanted sexual attention, or advances;
- trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, personal or political attacks, or sustained disruption;
- public or private harassment, intimidation, threats, or stalking;
- publishing another person's private information without explicit authorization;
- discriminatory conduct or exclusionary behavior;
- deliberate disclosure of credentials, vulnerabilities, confidential records, client data, personal data, or proprietary information;
- impersonation, falsification of approvals, misleading attribution, or manipulation of technical evidence;
- retaliation against a person who raises a good-faith concern;
- conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces and whenever an individual represents QUANTINOVA INDUSTRIES CORP or one of its projects. Project spaces include repositories, issues, pull requests, reviews, discussions, chats, meetings, events, and other official communication channels.

## Reporting

Report conduct concerns privately to `[CODE_OF_CONDUCT_CONTACT]`.

Do not include confidential details in a public issue. A report should include, when available:

- the date, time, and location of the incident;
- names or account identifiers of involved parties;
- a factual description of the behavior;
- links, screenshots, or other preserved evidence;
- relevant witnesses;
- any immediate safety or operational concern.

Reports will be handled as confidentially as reasonably possible. Information may be shared only with people who need it to assess, investigate, remediate, or satisfy legal obligations.

## Enforcement responsibilities

Project maintainers and designated corporate authorities are responsible for clarifying and enforcing standards of acceptable behavior. They may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, pull requests, and other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct, and will communicate moderation decisions when appropriate.

Enforcement decisions must be based on documented facts, proportionality, consistency, conflicts-of-interest controls, and applicable corporate policy.

## Enforcement guidelines

Maintainers may apply the following responses based on context, severity, history, and impact.

### 1. Correction

**Impact:** Use of inappropriate language or other conduct judged unprofessional or unwelcome.

**Response:** A private or public warning explaining the violation and required correction. A public apology or edited content may be required.

### 2. Warning

**Impact:** A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Response:** A formal warning with consequences for continued behavior. The person may be directed to stop contact with specified participants for a defined period.

### 3. Temporary restriction

**Impact:** A serious violation or sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Response:** Temporary suspension from interaction, contribution, review, or representation in project spaces. Violating the restriction may result in permanent removal.

### 4. Permanent removal

**Impact:** A pattern of violations, harassment, threats, disclosure of protected information, retaliation, or other severe misconduct.

**Response:** Permanent removal from project participation and, where applicable, escalation under corporate, contractual, or legal procedures.

## Appeals and conflicts of interest

A person subject to an enforcement action may submit a factual appeal to `[APPEAL_CONTACT]` within `[APPEAL_PERIOD]`. An appeal does not automatically suspend immediate protective measures.

A maintainer with a material conflict of interest must recuse from the investigation or decision.
